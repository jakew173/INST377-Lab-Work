My top 5 favorite albums

  1. Revolver - The Beatles: By 1966 the Beatles had fully merged popular music with psychedelia,
    incorportating world instruments, tape loops, backwards guitar solos, layered harmonies, and plenty of LSD.
    All of these factors together led to a historical album, the likes of which no kid in the mid 60's could have anticipated.
  2. Fear Fun - Father John Misty: A fantastical and occassionally pretentious look into the world of smoking, drinking,
    drug use, sexual escipades, romance, and art. Complete with biblical allegories, acoustic ballads, and a very misleading stage name.
  3. Congratulations - MGMT: Although MGMT is most known for easily recognizable Coachella hits, Congratulations
    provides a less pop-oriented approach, showcasing two close friends making pop music exactly how they want it: psychedelic,
    experimental, and without regard for a record label.
  4. The Life of Pablo - Kanye West: Although Kanye West has released plenty of other albums worthy of this spot on the list,
    The Life of Pablo gave listeners an unbridled, experimental, and incredibly intimate look into Kanye West's mind.
    This led for an erratic and somber journey into the world of genius production techniques and struggles with mental health.
  5. I'm in Your Mind Fuzz - King Gizzard & The Lizard Wizard: A group of best friends making manic garage rock just for the fun of it.
    An album just as jagged and whimsical as the band's name.
